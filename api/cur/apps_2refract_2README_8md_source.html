1 icedb-refract
2 ===============
3 
4 This program calculates the refractive index of a bulk medium, such as solid ice or liquid water.
5 Mixtures (and the effective medium approximation) will be handled in a separate application.
6 
7 How to run
8 --------------
9 
10 ###Command-line arguments:
11 
12 | Option | Required type | Description |
13 | ------ | ------------- | ----------- |
14 | --list-all | none | List all possible refractive index providers |
15 | --list-subst | string | List all refractive index providers for a given substance (viz. water, ice) |
16 | --subst | string | The substance of interest |
17 | --freq | double | Frequency |
18 | --freq-units | string | Units for the frequency (defaults to GHz) |
19 | --temp | double | Temperature |
20 | --temp-units | string | Units for the temperature (defaults to K) |
21 
22 The arguments are also positional, with ordering of substance, frequency, frequency units, temperature,
23 temperature units. The temperature and associated units are optional.
24 
25 **Note: when entering temperatures in degrees Celsius, negative temperatures cannot be specified using positional arguments.
26 In this case, you must specify temperature using ```--temp {temp}```**
27 
28 This works:
29 
30 ```icedb-refract ice 30 GHz 263 K```
31 
32 This also works:
33 
34 ```icedb-refract -f 18.7 GHz --subst ice```
35 
36 So does this:
37 
38 ```icedb-refract --subst water -T 10 --temp-units C -f 20 --freq-units um```
